FACILITIES

The College has great facilites that all students can use. This week we are focussing on:
  1. The Gym
  2. The LiteHouse
The College has a modern gym with lots of machines and weights such as Treadmels, Cross Trainers, Step Machines, MultiGyms and Bikes. The Gym is free to access for all students and is open untill 8AM-8PM week days.

The LITEhouse, which was established with funding made available from the European Regional Development Fund, is a Skills Development Scotland learning centre and provides high quality earning opportunities and support to students and staff. This guide gives important information on The LITEhouse and the resources and services provided by each of its sections:

College Learning Information Centre (CLIC)

The CLIC offers a substantial collection of printed and audio/visual resources to support the information, research and recreational needs of the College. Services provided by the CLIC include: online library catalogue called e-Library; computing facilities (including PCs with high speed Internet access, colour printers & scanners; wide range of online resources including e-books; study facilities; seminar room; photocopying (black & white and colour); binding (slide, comb and heat binding) and laminating (credit card size up to A3).

Flexible Learning Centre (FLC)

The Flexible Learning Centre offers an alternative to traditional classes so that you can improve your computing and other skills at a time and pace which suits you. We offer a wide range of computing courses to suit all abilities including a basic IT course, ECDL (European Computer Driving Licence), ECDL Advanced and various courses on Microsoft software applications. We also offer courses on SAGE Payroll & Accounts. Our team of tutors and administrative staff are on hand to help you with your coursework and any queries you may have.

Lifeskills Learning Unit

The Lifeskills Learning Unit offers you the opportunity to improve your confidence and ability in a number of essential “lifeskills” including: reading, writing, spelling, punctuation, IT skills as well as dealing with numbers and forms in everyday situations. There are no charges for using the Lifeskills Learning Unit and a learning plan, including when you will attend, is drawn up to meet your individual needs.
